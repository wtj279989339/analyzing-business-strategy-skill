# Slides Output Guide (.html and .pptx)

**When to use:** User selected `--format slides` or `--format pptx`

**Read this file:** Phase 5 (Deliverable Creation), before building the slides

**IMPORTANT: Always produce BOTH formats:**
1. **Slides** (pptx or html) — for presenting
   - Follows slide standards: modular, visual, concise
   - Good for presentations and quick scanning

2. **Markdown document** (.md) — for depth and reading
   - Follows document standards: linear narrative, detailed analysis, full evidence
   - Good for reading, understanding depth, sharing with stakeholders who want detail
   - This is ALWAYS produced alongside slides, even if user only asked for slides
   - See `output-documents.md` for document structure and standards

**File naming:** Use contextual, professional names based on the engagement topic:
- ✅ `Market_Entry_Strategy_EU_Paint_v1.md` and `Market_Entry_Strategy_EU_Paint_v1.pptx`
- ✅ `Pricing_Strategy_Analysis_CompanyX_v1.md` and `Pricing_Strategy_Analysis_CompanyX_v1.html`
- ❌ `report.md` and `slides.pptx` (too generic)

Use underscores or hyphens for file names, include version number (v1, v2, etc.), and keep naming consistent between the .md and slides files.

---

## Slides vs Documents: Key Difference

**Slides = Modular units.** Each slide is self-contained with one key idea. Visual impact and brevity are prioritized. The reader can jump around.

**Documents = Linear narrative.** Content flows continuously with depth and detail. The reader progresses sequentially.

This guide covers slides. For documents, see `output-documents.md`.

## Slide Structure (Non-Linear, Modular)

### Slide Types

#### 1. Title Slide
- Project title
- Date
- "Prepared for [client]"
- Clean, minimal design

#### 2. Executive Summary (1 slide)
- 5-15 key conclusions as bullets
- No charts — pure text for quick scanning
- Lead with the answer
- Each bullet = one major finding or recommendation

**Example:**
```
Executive Summary

[Client] should enter the EU/US B2C paint market via Amazon DTC with a phased launch:

• Market is attractive: EU decorative paint €12B, 3.2% CAGR, specialty eco segment growing 2x market rate

• Cost advantage survives: 38% manufacturing cost gap remains positive after tariffs, shipping, and compliance

• Amazon DTC is feasible: ~$180K launch cost, 14-month breakeven, based on 3 comparable case studies

• Phased approach recommended: UK first (Months 1-6), then Germany/France (Months 7-12)
```

#### 3. Basis of Perspectives (1 slide, optional for quick engagements)

**BCG pattern:** Show your work upfront. This builds credibility before any analysis.

**Content:**
- Number of sources analyzed (e.g., "12 public filings, 8 industry reports")
- Named databases and research firms used
- Case studies examined
- Any limitations (e.g., "no primary interviews conducted, model knowledge used for X")

**Example:**
```
Basis of our perspectives

• Secondary research
  - Euromonitor, Capital IQ, company filings
  - 8 industry reports (paint & coatings sector)
  - 12 competitor public filings (2022-2024)

• Case studies
  - 5 comparable market entries analyzed
  - 3 DTC launch case studies (detailed)

• Limitations
  - No primary consumer interviews conducted
  - US specialty segment size based on model estimates (±30% error)
  - Tariff rates current as of Jan 2025
```

**When to include:** Standard and deep engagements (3+ Business Experts, multiple source types). Skip for quick/executive-brief engagements where overhead isn't justified.

#### 4. Agenda/Roadmap (1 slide)
- Section overview
- Sets expectations
- Can be topic-only (exception to action-oriented headline rule)

#### 5. Section Dividers (1 per storyline)
- Clean, minimal
- Section title only
- Exempt from content density requirements
- Can be topic-only

#### 6. Evidence Slides (60-80% of deck)

**This is the core of your presentation.** Each evidence slide must have:

1. **Insight headline (action-oriented, not topic-only)**
   - ✅ "Stable market outlook and [Client]'s solid positioning enables solid base case in EU/NA"
   - ✅ "Retailers hold most of the power in the industry"
   - ✅ "Target market is $2B globally, essentially flat for 20+ years"
   - ❌ "Market Overview"
   - ❌ "Competitive Analysis"
   - ❌ "Financial Summary"

2. **2-3+ pieces of evidence**
   - Data points with sources
   - Expert quotes
   - Case study narratives
   - Qualitative observations
   - Logical reasoning chains

3. **Source footnote at bottom**
   - Every claim traceable
   - Format: "Source: Euromonitor 2025, Capital IQ"

**Chart OR text, not both competing for space.** If you have a chart, let it be the hero — minimal text. If you have text evidence, don't add a decorative chart.

**Example evidence slide (text-based):**
```
Headline: Specialty eco-friendly segment growing 2x market rate

• EU decorative paint market: €12B, 3.2% CAGR ('23-'30)
• Eco-friendly segment: 6.8% CAGR, reaching €2.1B by 2030
• Consumer survey (N=3,277): 68% willing to pay 15-20% premium for sustainable paint
• Regulatory tailwind: EU VOC limits tightening in 2026, favoring low-emission products

Source: Euromonitor 2025, Consumer survey (Jan 2025), EU Directive 2004/42/EC
```

**Example evidence slide (chart-based):**
```
Headline: Cost advantage survives tariffs and shipping

[Waterfall chart showing:]
Our COGS: $12.00
+ Tariff (15%): +$1.80
+ Shipping: +$8.50
+ Compliance: +$2.20
= Landed cost: $24.50

Competitor avg: $32.80
Our advantage: $8.30 (25%)

Source: Customs data, freight index, competitor filings
```

#### 7. Chart Slides

**Every chart must have:**
- Labeled axes with units
- One-line takeaway caption (what the chart proves)
- Source attribution
- Minimal text — let the chart speak

**Chart types to use:**
- **Bar charts:** Comparisons (our cost vs. competitors)
- **Line charts:** Trends over time (market growth, revenue trajectory)
- **Waterfall charts:** Financial breakdowns (cost buildup, P&L bridge)
- **Pie charts:** Market share, segmentation (use sparingly — bars often better)
- **Scatter plots:** Positioning maps (price vs. quality, market share vs. growth)
- **Tables:** Multi-dimensional comparisons (3-5 competitors × 3-5 attributes)

**BCG pattern:** Use specific numbers on charts. Label data points. Show CAGR prominently. Use color-coded growth indicators (green >2.5%, yellow 0-2.5%, red <0%).

#### 8. Recommendation Slides (2-3 slides)

**Content:**
- Specific, actionable next steps
- Prioritized (Phase 1 / Phase 2 or Immediate / Near-term)
- Timeline and budget
- Who owns each action

**Example:**
```
Headline: Phased launch approach minimizes risk and capital requirements

Phase 1: UK Market Entry (Months 1-6, ~$180K)
• Launch on Amazon UK with 3-5 SKUs
• Localize packaging and obtain UK compliance
• Run targeted PPC campaigns ($5K/month)
• Target: 500 units/month by Month 3

Phase 2: EU Expansion (Months 7-12, ~$120K)
• Add Germany and France with localized listings
• Leverage UK customer reviews for social proof
• Test retail partnership with 2-3 specialty chains
```

#### 9. Risks & Mitigations (1 slide, optional)

Only if decision carries meaningful downside. Not every deck needs this.

**Content:**
- What could go wrong (specific risks)
- Likelihood and impact
- How to mitigate
- Early warning indicators

#### 10. Appendix Slides (as needed)

- Detailed data tables
- Methodology
- Extended analysis
- Additional case studies

## Content Density Standards

**Target: 60-80% of slides should be "evidence slides"** (contain specific numbers, charts, quotes, qualitative findings, or sourced observations). The rest can be framing, transitions, or recommendations.

**Every evidence slide must have:**
1. Insight headline (action-oriented)
2. 2-3+ pieces of evidence (data points, quotes, case studies, logical reasoning)
3. Source footnote at bottom

**A slide with just a headline and 5 generic bullets is a pitch deck slide, not a consulting slide.** Add substance.

**Exempt from density requirements:**
- Title slide
- Agenda/roadmap
- Section dividers
- Appendix methodology slides

## Writing Style for Slides

Slides require brevity, visual impact, and clarity. Follow BCG patterns: action-oriented headlines, specific data, visual elements on every slide, and concise text.

### Slide Headlines: Complete Thoughts

Headlines should state a conclusion or insight, not just a topic. The headline alone should convey the key message.

**Do this:**
- "Cost advantage survives tariffs and shipping, positioning us competitively"
- "Specialty eco segment growing 2x market rate creates entry window"
- "Amazon DTC launch feasible under $500K with 14-month breakeven"

**Don't do this:**
- "Cost Analysis"
- "Market Growth"
- "Channel Strategy"

### Bullet Points: Specific and Sourced

Every bullet should contain specific data with sources in parentheses. Keep bullets concise — no more than 2 lines per bullet.

**Do this:**
- "EU decorative paint: €12B, 3.2% CAGR ('23-'30) (Euromonitor 2025)"
- "Eco segment: 6.8% CAGR, reaching €2.1B by 2030 — 2x overall market (Euromonitor 2025)"
- "Consumer survey: 68% willing to pay 15-20% premium for sustainable paint (N=3,277, Jan 2025)"

**Don't do this:**
- "Market is growing — Euromonitor 2025" (vague, dash format)
- "Eco segment has potential based on industry reports" (no specific data, no source)
- "Consumers want sustainable options according to various surveys we reviewed" (too long, vague)

### Text Limits: Maximum 4 Lines

No paragraph or text block should exceed 4 lines. If content is longer, split into multiple bullets or create a second slide.

**Do this (3 lines):**
> The EU decorative paint market is €12B, growing at 3.2% CAGR from 2023 to 2030. The specialty eco-friendly segment is growing at 6.8% CAGR — more than double the overall market rate — creating a window for new entrants.

**Don't do this (6 lines):**
> The EU decorative paint market is €12B and growing at 3.2% CAGR from 2023 to 2030, driven primarily by the specialty eco-friendly segment which is growing at 6.8% CAGR — more than double the overall market rate. This creates a window for new entrants who can credibly position in the sustainable segment, as incumbents' reformulation cycles typically take 2-3 years, giving early movers time to establish brand presence before competitive response.

### Visual Elements: Every Slide Needs One

Following BCG patterns, every content slide must have a visual element — chart, table, icon, image, or diagram. Text-only slides are not acceptable.

**Visual options:**
- **Charts**: Bar charts for comparisons, line charts for trends, waterfall charts for financial breakdowns
- **Tables**: Multi-dimensional comparisons (3-5 competitors × 3-5 dimensions)
- **Icons**: In colored circles next to section headers or key points
- **Images**: Product photos, market examples, case study visuals
- **Diagrams**: Process flows, decision trees, positioning maps
- **Large stat callouts**: 60-72pt numbers with small labels

**BCG pattern reference:**
Read `references/methodology/bcg-patterns.md` for specific examples of:
- Positioning maps with named competitors
- Waterfall charts for P&L bridges
- "From... To..." transformation slides
- Lever-based transformation frameworks
- Color-coded growth indicators (green >2.5%, yellow 0-2.5%, red <0%)

### Implications: Explicit, Not Implied

After presenting data, explicitly state what it means for the client. Don't assume the reader will connect the dots.

**Do this:**
> **Data:** Our landed cost is $24.50 vs. competitor average of $32.80
>
> **Implication:** This $8.30 (25%) advantage allows us to price at $40-50 — above mass market ($20-30) but below premium ($60+) — capturing margin while remaining accessible to price-conscious eco-buyers.

**Don't do this:**
> **Data:** Our landed cost is $24.50 vs. competitor average of $32.80
>
> [No implication — reader has to figure out what this means]

### Source Footnotes: Specific and Retrievable

Every evidence slide must have source attribution at the bottom. Use specific source names with URLs where possible.

**Do this:**
- "Source: Euromonitor 2025, Capital IQ, Consumer survey (Jan 2025, N=3,277)"
- "Source: Home Depot IR (https://ir.homedepot.com/behr-partnership)"

**Don't do this:**
- "Source: Industry reports"
- "Source: Market research"

## Format-Specific Guidance

### HTML Slides (.html)

**Nested skill:** `skills/frontend-slides/SKILL.md` — **Read this file first** to understand capabilities.

**Key capabilities:**
- Chart.js for interactive charts (bar, line, pie, scatter, radar)
- Reveal.js for presentation framework
- Inline SVG for custom visualizations
- Animations and transitions
- Runs in browser, zero dependencies
- Single HTML file with embedded CSS/JS

**CRITICAL from frontend-slides skill: Viewport Fitting**

Every slide MUST fit exactly within the viewport. No scrolling within slides, ever.

**Content density limits per slide (from frontend-slides skill):**

| Slide Type | Maximum Content |
|------------|-----------------|
| Title/Section | Title + subtitle only |
| Text-heavy | 5-7 bullets OR 2-3 paragraphs (max 150 words) |
| Chart + text | Chart + 3-4 bullets max |
| Chart only | Chart + title + source |
| Two-column | 4-5 bullets per column |
| Image + text | Image + 3-4 bullets |

**If content overflows → split into multiple slides.** Never scroll within a slide.

**Design principles (from frontend-slides skill):**
- Pick a bold, content-informed color palette (not generic blue)
- Dominance over equality (one color 60-70% visual weight)
- Dark/light contrast (dark backgrounds for title/conclusion, light for content)
- Commit to a visual motif (rounded frames, icons in circles, thick borders)
- Every slide needs a visual element (image, chart, icon, shape)
- Typography: 36-44pt slide titles, 20-24pt section headers, 14-16pt body
- 0.5" minimum margins, 0.3-0.5" between content blocks

**Avoid (from frontend-slides skill):**
- Don't repeat the same layout
- Don't center body text (left-align paragraphs)
- Don't default to blue (pick colors that reflect the topic)
- Don't create text-only slides
- NEVER use accent lines under titles (hallmark of AI-generated slides)

**QA (REQUIRED from frontend-slides skill):**
- Open the HTML file in a browser
- Check every slide for viewport fitting (no scrolling)
- Verify charts render correctly
- Test keyboard navigation (arrow keys)
- Check on different screen sizes if possible

### PowerPoint (.pptx)

**Nested skill:** `skills/pptx/SKILL.md` — **Read this file first** to understand capabilities.

**Key capabilities:**
- pptxgenjs library for generation
- Chart objects (bar, line, pie, scatter)
- Tables with formatting
- Text boxes with positioning
- Traditional slide deck format
- Editable by user after generation

**Critical rules (from pptx skill):**
- Pick a bold, content-informed color palette (see pptx skill for 10 palette options)
- Dominance over equality (one color dominates 60-70%)
- Dark/light contrast (dark for title/conclusion, light for content)
- Every slide needs a visual element (image, chart, icon, shape)
- Typography: 36-44pt slide titles, 20-24pt section headers, 14-16pt body
- 0.5" minimum margins, 0.3-0.5" between content blocks
- Never repeat the same layout across slides
- Don't center body text (left-align)
- Don't create text-only slides
- NEVER use accent lines under titles

**Design ideas (from pptx skill):**
- Two-column (text left, illustration right)
- Icon + text rows (icon in colored circle, bold header, description)
- 2x2 or 2x3 grid (image on one side, content blocks on other)
- Half-bleed image (full left/right side) with content overlay
- Large stat callouts (60-72pt numbers with small labels)
- Comparison columns (before/after, pros/cons)
- Timeline or process flow (numbered steps, arrows)

**QA (REQUIRED from pptx skill):**

Assume there are problems. Your first render is almost never correct.

1. **Content QA:**
   ```bash
   python -m markitdown output.pptx
   ```
   Check for missing content, typos, wrong order, leftover placeholder text.

2. **Visual QA (USE SUBAGENTS):**
   Convert to images:
   ```bash
   python scripts/office/soffice.py --headless --convert-to pdf output.pptx
   pdftoppm -jpeg -r 150 output.pdf slide
   ```

   Spawn a subagent to inspect `slide-01.jpg`, `slide-02.jpg`, etc. for:
   - Overlapping elements
   - Text overflow or cut off
   - Low-contrast text/icons
   - Uneven gaps
   - Elements too close (<0.3" gaps)
   - Insufficient margins (<0.5")
   - Leftover placeholder content

3. **Verification loop:**
   - Generate → Convert to images → Inspect
   - List issues found
   - Fix issues
   - Re-verify affected slides
   - Repeat until clean

**Do not declare success until you've completed at least one fix-and-verify cycle.**

## Reading from YAML Files

**Source of truth:** All expert findings are in `process/workstream-*.yaml` files.

**How to use them:**

1. **Read all YAML files** in `process/` directory
2. **Organize by storyline, not by expert** — multiple experts may contribute to the same storyline
3. **Extract:**
   - `key_findings` → slide headlines and evidence
   - `data_points` → quantitative data for charts
   - `implications` → "so what for client" (can be a callout or separate slide)
   - `charts_suggested` → visualizations to include (use these!)
   - `case_studies` → examples for evidence slides
   - `benchmarking` → competitor comparison tables
4. **Preserve source citations** — every slide has source footnote
5. **Check confidence levels** — `data_gaps` field lists missing data, flag these

**Don't just copy-paste YAML content.** Synthesize findings into slide-appropriate format:
- One key idea per slide
- Brevity over detail (save detail for appendix)
- Visual over text when possible

**Use suggested charts from YAML `charts_suggested` field.** Experts identified where data would be more compelling as visualization — honor that.

## Benchmarking Standards

**Always use named competitors, not "industry peers."** Identify 3-5 specific companies.

**Multi-dimensional benchmarking table (required for at least one comparison):**

Create a table slide with 3-5 competitors × 3-5 dimensions:

```
Headline: [Client] positioned between mass market and premium players

| Competitor | Market Share | Pricing | Distribution | Recent Moves |
|------------|--------------|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Competitor A | 18% (€2.2B) | Premium ($50-60) | Retail 70%, DTC 30% | Launched eco line 2024 |
| Competitor B | 12% (€1.4B) | Mass ($20-30) | Retail 95%, B2B 5% | Acquired distributor 2023 |
| Competitor C | 8% (€960M) | Mid-tier ($35-45) | DTC 60%, Retail 40% | Expanded to US 2024 |
| [Client] | New entrant | Target: $40-50 | DTC (Amazon) | — |

Implications: Our $40-50 target pricing positions us in the mid-tier gap between mass market and premium. Focus on quality differentiation to justify premium over mass market players.

Source: Company filings, Euromonitor 2025
```

**After every benchmark, add "Implications to [client]" — not just "Competitor X has 47% margin."**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Topic-only headlines** — "Market Overview" instead of "Market is attractive for new entrants"
2. **Sparse slides** — headline + 2 generic bullets with no evidence
3. **Text-only slides** — no charts, images, or visual elements
4. **Data without implications** — presenting numbers without saying what they mean
5. **Generic competitors** — "industry peers" instead of "Uniqlo, Heilan, Nike"
6. **No "Basis of Perspectives"** — skipping the credibility slide (for standard+ engagements)
7. **Ignoring suggested charts** — YAML files have `charts_suggested` field, use it
8. **Viewport overflow (HTML)** — slides that require scrolling
9. **No visual QA (PPTX)** — declaring success without converting to images and inspecting
10. **Repeating same layout** — every slide looks identical
11. **Accent lines under titles** — hallmark of AI-generated slides, avoid
12. **Low-contrast text/icons** — light text on light backgrounds
13. **Orphan evidence** — interesting data that doesn't support any recommendation
14. **No synthesis** — just concatenating expert YAML files without narrative arc

## Final Note

Slides are about visual impact and modularity. Unlike documents (which are linear and detailed), slides allow you to present one key idea at a time with supporting evidence.

**One slide = one idea.** If you're trying to fit two ideas on one slide, split it.

**Show, don't tell.** Use charts and visuals wherever possible. A well-designed chart is worth a thousand words.

**Every slide must justify its existence.** If a slide doesn't advance the storyline or support a recommendation, cut it.

The reader should be able to follow your logic from problem → evidence → conclusion → recommendation by reading slide headlines alone.
